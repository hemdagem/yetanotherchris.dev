This list is based off Jon's Skeet alternative CV strategy. It's basically my core beliefs as a software developer: I've borrowed and adapted a few of his, but the majority are my own. Hopefully it doesn't read as too self-righteous or pretentious.

Beliefs

Testing

  • I write test driven code, but realise you can write code-first.
  • I know from experience however that writing tests last is often very boring.

Clean code

  • I believe you should keep the camp fire cleaner than when you first got there.
  • I prefer code which can be read easily over code which runs 5% faster but no-one else understands
  • I prefer 3 liners with many variables to 120-character one liners.
  • I don't assume my code is perfect.

Learning

  • I'll happily use an answer I find via Google than spend 3 hours solving it myself.
  • I also realise Google search doesn't always hold the key, or as Abraham Lincoln once said, "Don't believe everything you see on the Internet."
  • If I don't understand something I like to spend my spare time learning about it - I love to learn new techniques and technologies.
  • I would rather use a new technology on a personal project first than have to perform a U-turn at work.
  • I prefer keen developers with much to learn over experienced developers who feel they have nothing to learn

Work-life balance

  • I would choose happy developers over slightly less productive developers. Happy devs bring passion to whatever they do.
  • Estimates should account for the developers only working 60% of their working day (Mike Cohn).
  • I prefer going home at 5 to sleep on a problem over staying at the office until midnight and then being useless the next day.
  • I would always choose a ROWE to clock watching.

Power

  • I prefer agency and trust over command and control.
  • I believe developers work best when they're empowered rather than told what to do and how to do it.
  • I believe in collective ownership and responsibility rather than blame culture.

Peer reviews

  • I like pair programming, one person never knows all the answers, or will catch their own errors.
  • I prefer pairing to code reviews, this is the best form of peer-review.
  • I believe every level of programmer and analyst benefits from being peer reviewed.
  • I prefer code reviews which force me to change my design over reviews which stroke my ego.

Mistakes

  • I believe if someone has made a mistake you are wiser to tell them in person than infront of a group.
  • I prefer suggestions for improvements over code-shaming.
  • If somebody's doesn't have your specialist knowledge, teach them rather than patronise them.

Upfront design and documentation

  • I believe you will never create something correctly after one try.
  • I prefer whiteboards over Excel, Word or Visio.
  • I choose Markdown to any other documentation format.
  • I prefer progress over process.
  • I think one person prototyping a solution for a 3 hours and presenting it is more efficient than six people discussing it for 1 hour.

Collaboration

  • I prefer Github issues for technical discussions before booking a meeting.
  • I prefer close collaboration over the heroic coder mentality
  • I keen to deferentiate between heroic coder and someone who is really pro-active. Sometimes they appear the same but one is aloof, the other is trying to help the team.
